MOMO Fest
Melbourne '26

Two days. One park.
Thirty-plus kitchens. Endless momo.

A free, family-friendly cultural and culinary festival that celebrated MOMO — the beloved Nepali and Himalayan dumpling — alongside the music, dance and food of multicultural Melbourne.

What was on

Two days,
plenty to chew on.

Across the weekend, MOMO Fest 2026 ran a steady rotation of music, performance, family activities and — the heart of it — a great deal of momo. Highlights of the 2026 programme:

01 ·

MOMO & food vendors

30+ Nepali, Himalayan and multicultural food stalls served everything from steamed and pan-fried momo to thukpa, sel roti, chaat, dumplings and beyond.

02 ·

Live music & performance

Two stages ran across the weekend featuring Nepali, Tibetan, Bhutanese, Indian and Australian artists, with roving performers and dance throughout the park.

03 ·

Family & community

Children's activities, amusement rides, games, market stalls and a community space — designed for groups, families and first-time visitors alike.

04 ·

Licensed bar

A licensed bar ran alongside non-alcoholic drinks and chiya (Nepali milk tea). All beverages were sold by licensed vendors.

05 ·

Cultural showcase

A daily showcase highlighted Nepali and Himalayan craft, dress and ritual — alongside multicultural Australian artists and makers.

06 ·

Accessibility

Footscray Park is largely flat with accessible paths, public toilets and easy public transport — well suited to a free public festival of this scale.

Footscray Park sits on Wurundjeri Country alongside the Maribyrnong River — minutes from the city by train, with the Footscray Market and Little Saigon a short walk away.
